<inclusion_criteria>
Prioritize papers that meet ONE or more of these criteria AND would make a patient or caregiver say: "This helps me understand what is going wrong, what might help, or whether the field is genuinely moving."

1. **Direct Mechanistic Progress**:
   - Human ME/CFS or clearly ME/CFS-like post-viral cohorts showing a concrete disease mechanism, causal pathway, or strong mechanistic model.
   - Especially valuable: studies connecting immune dysfunction, endothelial/microvascular problems, mitochondrial or metabolic failure, autonomic dysfunction, viral persistence/reactivation, BBB/neuroinflammation, autoantibodies, or exertion-triggered abnormalities to a potentially targetable process.
   - Prefer studies with perturbation, replication, tissue-level evidence, exertion/provocation, or a clear bridge from mechanism to therapy/diagnostics.

2. **Actionable Treatments or Strong Therapeutic Signals**:
   - Interventional studies, pilot trials, high-quality observational treatment studies, or unusually informative patient-reported treatment datasets.
   - Include repurposed drugs, immune modulation, antivirals, anticoagulation/microcirculation approaches, autonomic treatments, or other interventions that could plausibly change disease biology or materially improve function.
   - Include major upcoming trials only if they test a serious disease-modifying or diagnostic-enabling intervention and are likely to matter to the field.

3. **Diagnostics & Testability**:
   - Biomarker or diagnostic studies that move beyond "patients differ from controls" toward reproducible classification, validation, severity stratification, provocation response, or near-term test development.
   - Provocation studies (e.g., CPET, tilt, repeat exertion, orthostatic challenge) are high priority when they objectively capture PEM, autonomic failure, or impaired energy production.

4. **Biologically Meaningful Subtyping**:
   - Machine learning, cluster analysis, or multi-omic stratification that identifies subgroups with mechanistic, prognostic, or treatment relevance.
   - Symptom clustering alone is NOT enough unless it clearly improves trial design, case finding, or treatment matching.

5. **High-Relevance Long COVID Overlap**:
   - Long COVID studies are in scope when they strongly overlap with ME/CFS biology or symptom structure, especially PEM, orthostatic intolerance/POTS, neuroimmune dysfunction, exertion intolerance, endothelial injury, or shared therapeutic hypotheses.
   - They can also be included when they clearly accelerate the broader post-viral field in a way that plausibly benefits ME/CFS patients, even if the overlap is somewhat indirect.

6. **Strict Case Definitions & Cohort Quality**:
   - Strong preference for CCC, ICC, IOM/NAM, expert clinical diagnosis, or well-characterized post-viral cohorts with PEM-rich phenotypes.

7. **Field Momentum, Hope, and Institutional Progress**:
   - Include high-signal news, conference reports, project announcements, funding wins, major reviews, and strategic overviews when they materially show that the field is moving.
   - This is especially relevant when the item:
     - announces a serious biomarker or stratification initiative
     - summarizes multiple promising treatment directions
     - documents growing recognition by researchers, institutions, or funders
     - gives patients and caregivers a realistic sense that progress is being organized rather than stalled
   - These items do NOT need to be primary mechanistic papers if they provide meaningful orientation or hope grounded in real research progress.
</inclusion_criteria>

<exclusion_criteria>
Reject papers that meet ANY of these criteria:

1. **Psychosomatic/Behavioral Models**:
   - Framing ME/CFS/Long COVID as "functional neurologic disorder" (FND), "bodily distress syndrome", or primarily driven by "fear avoidance", deconditioning, or "unhelpful beliefs".

2. **Graded Exercise Therapy (GET) & CBT for "Cure"**:
   - Studies promoting GET or "reconditioning" as a cure.
   - Studies promoting CBT or "Lightning Process" as curative for the underlying disease.
   - *Exception*: Studies *measuring* PEM via CPET are ACCEPTABLE. Studies *managing* mental health (coping) are acceptable if they don't claim to cure the physical disease.

3. **Vague Criteria**:
   - Studies using the "Oxford criteria" (too broad, includes primary depression).

4. **Generic Signal Without Actionable Progress**:
   - Cross-sectional biomarker papers that only show a difference between patients and controls without replication, validation, mechanistic depth, or diagnostic usefulness.
   - Generic omics screens, cytokine panels, microbiome shifts, or autoantibody associations that do not materially narrow the field or point to a testable target.

5. **Reviews / Commentary / News With Low Incremental Value**:
   - Reject reviews, commentary, news, and project items only when they are generic, repetitive, vague, or mostly policy/awareness framing without concrete research value.
   - Keep reviews or news if they materially help patients understand the treatment landscape, mechanistic direction, or institutional momentum.
   - Systematic reviews/meta-analyses should be rejected only when they add little beyond broad confirmation or summarize weak/supportive interventions without sharpening the field.

6. **Descriptive Epidemiology Without Strategic Value**:
   - Prevalence, incidence, burden, or broad risk-factor studies that do not improve diagnosis, stratification, mechanism selection, or trial design.
   - Symptom surveys without mechanistic correlation or actionable subgrouping.

7. **Broad Long COVID Content That Is Too Indirect**:
   - Long COVID papers focused on general rehabilitation, nutrition, lifestyle, mental health, or broad symptom management without a strong ME/CFS-like biology, clear treatment signal, or useful field-orientation value.
   - Long COVID studies dominated by respiratory, general recovery, or heterogeneous chronic symptoms should usually be rejected unless they clearly shape diagnostics, treatment strategy, or the broader post-viral research agenda.

8. **Weak or Distant Therapeutic Relevance**:
   - Supportive-care-only papers, generic lifestyle advice, non-specific supplement reviews, and rehabilitation protocols unless they contain unusually strong controlled evidence or are directly relevant to severe ME/CFS care.
   - Be especially skeptical of herbal mixes, traditional medicine formulas, low-plausibility natural products, and alternative-medicine style intervention papers unless the evidence is unusually strong and directly relevant.
   - Trial protocols for minor/supportive interventions or papers with no results and little field-shaping importance.

9. **Too Early / Too Indirect Models**:
   - Animal-only, cell-line-only, or analogy papers without a convincing bridge back to human ME/CFS or a highly plausible therapeutic target.
   - Adjacent-disease papers should NOT be rejected automatically. Keep them if they offer a compelling mechanistic or therapeutic bridge that patients would plausibly care about.
   - Case reports of unrelated diseases (e.g., West Nile, MOGAD, MS) should usually be rejected unless they introduce a novel mechanism or treatment idea directly applicable to ME/CFS.
</exclusion_criteria>

<input_analysis_tips>
- **Patient/Caregiver Lens**: "Interesting to researchers" is NOT sufficient. The paper should improve understanding, testability, treatment hope, or confidence that the field is advancing in a serious way.
- **News / Reviews**: Do NOT auto-reject. Ask whether the item helps patients orient themselves: What is promising? What is being funded? Which directions look real? Which are hype?
- **Long COVID**: Be more permissive than before if the paper clearly strengthens the broader post-viral research map, even when the ME/CFS overlap is somewhat indirect.
- **Biomarkers**: Prefer validation, replication, provocation, tissue specificity, or direct links to symptoms/treatment targets. Penalize one-off correlation papers.
- **Machine Learning**: Include ML-based subtyping only if it is biologically grounded or useful for treatment matching or trial stratification.
- **Alternative Medicine / Herbals**: Be skeptical of Chinese medicine, herbal formulas, vague supplement stacks, and low-plausibility "natural" therapies unless there is unusually strong evidence.
- **Landmark Discipline**: Be skeptical. Very few papers are truly high-impact.
</input_analysis_tips>
