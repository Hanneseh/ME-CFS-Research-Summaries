### 🎯 2025-10-05 - Research Roundup

**I. Evolution of Disease Understanding**
The scientific consensus on ME/CFS and Long COVID is solidifying around a model of a post-infectious neuroimmune disease driven by a combination of genetic predisposition, persistent immune dysregulation, and profound metabolic disruption. Landmark genetic research provides a firm biological foundation, linking ME/CFS risk to specific genes involved in the immune response to infection and definitively separating it from depression [2025-08-06](#2025-08-06-initial-findings-from-the-decodeme-genome-wide-association-study-of-myalgic-encephalomyelitischronic-fatigue-syndrome). This immune dysfunction manifests as a heightened innate immune response [2025-09-03](#2025-09-03-heightened-innate-immunity-may-trigger-chronic-inflammation-fatigue-and-post-exertional-malaise-in-mecfs), sustained activation of inflammatory pathways like the NLRP3 inflammasome [2025-09-08](#2025-09-08-the-nlrp3-inflammasome-as-a-key-pathway-in-the-affective-and-chronic-fatigue-symptoms-of-long-covid), and an autoimmune component where antibodies may attack the body's own tissues [2025-09-19](#2025-09-19-exploratory-study-on-autoantibodies-to-arginine-rich-human-peptides-mimicking-epstein-barr-virus-in-women-with-post-covid-and-myalgic-encephalomyelitischronic-fatigue-syndrome) [2025-09-09](#2025-09-09-anti-neuronal-and-anti-mitochondrial-autoantibodies-are-associated-with-lower-functional-status-and-more-severe-respiratory-symptoms-in-post-covid-syndrome). In fact, patient-derived IgG antibodies have been shown to directly enter healthy cells and cause mitochondrial fragmentation, providing a mechanistic link between the immune system and the disease's hallmark energy impairment [2025-08-10](#2025-08-10-mecfs-and-pasc-patient-derived-immunoglobulin-complexes-disrupt-mitochondrial-function-and-alter-inflammatory-marker-secretion). This leads to a vicious cycle where microvascular dysfunction in skeletal muscle causes an overload of ions like sodium [2022-12-09](#2022-12-09-muscle-sodium-content-in-patients-with-myalgic-encephalomyelitischronic-fatigue-syndrome), triggering mitochondrial damage that worsens with exertion and establishes the chronic nature of the illness [2024-11-06](#2024-11-06-key-pathophysiological-role-of-skeletal-muscle-disturbance-in-post-covid-and-myalgic-encephalomyelitischronic-fatigue-syndrome-accumulated-evidence). These peripheral issues converge on the central nervous system, where neuroinflammation is thought to activate primitive "sickness behavior" circuits, causing symptoms like PEM, brain fog, and sensory overload [2025-08-19](#2025-08-19-causes-of-symptoms-and-symptom-persistence-in-long-covid-and-myalgic-encephalomyelitischronic-fatigue-syndrome) [2022-05-09](#2022-05-09-the-pathobiology-of-myalgic-encephalomyelitischronic-fatigue-syndrome-the-case-for-neuroglial-failure).

**II. Evolution in Diagnostics**
The search for objective biomarkers is shifting from static measurements to dynamic tests that capture the functional impairments of ME/CFS. Provocation with exercise is proving to be a powerful tool, revealing specific epigenetic changes in DNA [2025-09-03](#2025-09-03-precision-medicine-study-of-post-exertional-malaise-epigenetic-changes-in-myalgic-encephalomyelitischronic-fatigue-patients-during-exercise) and profound disruptions in metabolic recovery that are unique to patients and provide a molecular basis for post-exertional malaise [2022-03-31](#2022-03-31-plasma-metabolomics-reveals-disrupted-response-and-recovery-following-maximal-exercise-in-myalgic-encephalomyelitis-chronic-fatigue-syndrome). Promising non-invasive "liquid biopsy" techniques are also emerging, such as analyzing circulating cell-free RNA (cfRNA), which has identified a distinct immune signature of T-cell exhaustion and chronic inflammation in patient blood [2025-08-11](#2025-08-11-circulating-cell-free-rna-signatures-for-the-characterization-and-diagnosis-of-myalgic-encephalomyelitischronic-fatigue-syndrome). Another innovative approach uses microfluidics to show that red blood cells from ME/CFS patients have an impaired ability to speed up in response to low oxygen, a finding that could explain systemic oxygen delivery issues and has been developed into a machine learning model with high diagnostic specificity [2025-08-11](#2025-08-11-microfluidic-assessment-of-po2-regulated-rbc-capillary-velocity-in-mecfs). Other specific protein biomarkers like haptoglobin phenotypes, which correlate with PEM severity [2025-08-28](#2025-08-28-haptoglobin-phenotypes-and-structural-variants-associate-with-post-exertional-malaise-and-cognitive-dysfunction-in-myalgic-encephalomyelitis), and SMPDL3B, which indicates subclinical kidney stress and can stratify patients into distinct subgroups [2025-09-12](#2025-09-12-circulating-levels-of-smpdl3b-define-metabolic-endophenotypes-and-subclinical-kidney-alterations-in-myalgic-encephalomyelitis), are further advancing the move toward a multi-marker, personalized diagnostic framework. For Long COVID, PET imaging that detects increased brain-wide AMPA receptors has been shown to distinguish patients with cognitive impairment from controls with very high accuracy [2025-10-01](#2025-10-01-systemic-increase-of-ampa-receptors-associated-with-cognitive-impairment-of-long-covid).

**III. Evolution in Finding a Cure**
Therapeutic strategies are increasingly targeting the core autoimmune and metabolic dysfunctions identified in mechanistic research. The most promising results have come from immunotherapies, including a pilot study of the anti-CD38 antibody daratumumab 💊, which produced major, sustained improvements in a majority of ME/CFS patients by targeting antibody-producing plasma cells [2025-07-09](#2025-07-09-plasma-cell-targeting-with-the-anti-cd38-antibody-daratumumab-in-myalgic-encephalomyelitischronic-fatigue-syndrome-a-clinical-pilot-study). Similarly, immunoadsorption 💊, a procedure to filter autoantibodies from the blood, improved physical function in a subset of post-COVID ME/CFS patients [2023-10-09](#2023-10-09-observational-study-of-repeat-immunoadsorption-ria-in-post-covid-mecfs-patients-with-elevated-%C3%9F2-adrenergic-receptor-autoantibodiesan-interim-report). Broader immunomodulators like low-dose naltrexone (LDN) 💊 and low-dose aripiprazole 💊 are also reported as beneficial [2025-09-10](#2025-09-10-effect-of-low-dose-naltrexone-for-long-covid-a-systematic-review) [2024-04-08](#2024-04-08-longitudinal-cytokine-and-multi-modal-health-data-of-an-extremely-severe-mecfs-patient-with-hsd-reveals-insights-into-immunopathology-and-disease-severity). On the metabolic front, the mTOR inhibitor rapamycin 💊 showed promise in improving autophagy and reducing fatigue [2025-10-21](#2025-10-21-low-dose-rapamycin-alleviates-clinical-symptoms-of-fatigue-and-pem-in-mecfs-patients-via-improvement-of-autophagy-a-pilot-study), while metformin 💊 has been shown to prevent Long COVID and has a strong rationale for treating ME/CFS [2023-06-08](#2023-06-08-outpatient-treatment-of-covid-19-and-incidence-of-post-covid-19-condition-over-10-months-covid-out-a-multicentre-randomised-quadruple-blind-parallel-group-phase-3-trial) [2025-08-07](#2025-08-07-a-perspective-on-the-role-of-metformin-in-treating-myalgic-encephalomyelitischronic-fatigue-syndrome-mecfs-and-long-covid). Other interventions showing benefit include pyridostigmine 💊 for improving muscle strength and autonomic function [2025-09-03](#2025-09-03-pyridostigmine-improves-hand-grip-strength-in-patients-with-myalgic-encephalomyelitischronic-fatigue-syndrome), oxaloacetate 💊 for cognitive function [2025-09-23](#2025-09-23-relationships-between-fatigue-cognitive-function-and-upright-activity-in-a-randomized-trial-of-oxaloacetate-for-myalgic-encephalomyelitischronic-fatigue-syndrome), the wakefulness agent solriamfetol 💊 [2025-09-16](#2025-09-16-solriamfetol-improves-daily-fatigue-symptoms-in-adults-with-myalgic-encephalomyelitischronic-fatigue-syndrome-after-8-weeks-of-treatment), and H.E.L.P. apheresis 💊, a blood-filtering procedure that dramatically improved Long COVID symptoms [2023-05-26](#2023-05-26-long-covid-patients-successfully-treated-by-means-of-heparin-mediated-extracorporeal-ldl-precipitation-help-apheresis).
