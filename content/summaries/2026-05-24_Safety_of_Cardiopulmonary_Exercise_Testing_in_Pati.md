## Summary

This study provides crucial clinical reassurance for patients and healthcare providers managing severe post-COVID-19 condition. It offers high-resolution, systematic evidence showing that while a single CPET triggers post-exertional malaise, this flare is temporary and safely resolves within 4 to 7 days. These findings validate CPET as a safe objective diagnostic tool that does not cause permanent clinical worsening, helping patients document their physiological limitations with confidence.

## What was researched?

This matched case-control study investigated the safety and feasibility of cardiopulmonary exercise testing (CPET) in patients with severe post-COVID-19 condition. Specifically, the researchers monitored how a single maximal CPET impacted the severity and duration of post-exertional malaise (PEM).

## Why was it researched?

Although CPET is the gold standard for objectively assessing functional impairment and physiological limitations in post-viral illnesses, concerns remain about its safety. Systematic evidence regarding the exact recovery timeline and symptom burden following exercise testing in this patient group has been sparse.

## How was it researched?

The study evaluated 18 patients with severe post-COVID-19 condition and 18 healthy controls matched for sex, age, and body mass index. Participants completed a single maximal symptom-limited CPET on a bicycle ergometer. Ten PEM-specific symptoms were tracked daily via a digital diary for 7 days before and 14 days after the test, alongside baseline clinical questionnaires.

## What has been found?

PCC patients demonstrated severely reduced physical capacity compared to controls, with lower peak power output and peak oxygen consumption. While patients experienced a significant temporary increase in overall symptom severity 1 to 3 days post-CPET, symptoms predictably returned to baseline levels within 4 to 7 days. Fatigue and joint pain were the only individual symptoms that significantly increased in the acute phase, with no permanent baseline changes.

## Discussion

The strengths of this study include its rigorous, daily tracking of multiple PEM symptoms before and after provocation. However, the study is limited by its relatively small sample size and the exclusion of very severe, bedbound patients who are unable to perform a bicycle ergometer test.

## Conclusion & Future Work

The researchers concluded that a single maximal CPET is a feasible and safe diagnostic tool for characterizing functional impairment in severe post-COVID-19 condition. Pacing and symptom-adapted management are recommended during and after the assessment.
