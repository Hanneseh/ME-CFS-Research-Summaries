## Summary

This conference summary delivers a sobering but scientifically essential reality check to patients and caregivers by documenting the failure of several major, unstratified clinical trials. It establishes that ME/CFS is highly complex and unlikely to respond to one-size-fits-all therapies, shifting the field's clinical momentum toward personalized, biomarker-guided medicine. Crucially, the discovery of brain-specific genetic risk signals and peripheral muscle tissue alterations provides patients with validated biological pathways, reinforcing the physiological reality of their illness while pointing researchers toward more precise drug targets.

## What was researched?

This article summarizes the primary research presentations and clinical trial outcomes from the fourth International ME/CFS Conference held in Berlin on May 7-8, 2026. The conference focused on advancements in genetics, neuroimaging, biomarkers, and therapeutic interventions for myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome (ME/CFS) and Long COVID.

## Why was it researched?

The summary was published to provide the patient and scientific communities with an objective, consolidated overview of the latest international scientific momentum. Understanding where clinical trials failed and where basic biological research succeeded is essential for refining future therapeutic strategies and diagnostic subtyping.

## How was it researched?

This conference report synthesizes findings from over 50 international experts, highlighting genetic data from the DecodeME cohort, whole-body TSPO PET imaging from Stanford, and multiple clinical trial datasets. These clinical trials included a randomized, placebo-controlled trial of low-dose naltrexone with 160 patients, a sham-controlled immunoadsorption trial from Charité, and a clinical pilot study of an anti-CD38 monoclonal antibody.

## What has been found?

The genetic analysis showed ME/CFS heritability is exclusively enriched in brain tissues, specifically highlighting a rare striatal cell type called eccentric medium spiny neurons (eMSNs). In contrast, several highly anticipated controlled trials—including low-dose naltrexone, immunoadsorption, and methylprednisolone—failed to meet their primary efficacy endpoints. However, a clinical pilot trial utilizing daratumumab 💊 reported clinical response in 6 out of 10 patients alongside alterations in their autoantibody profiles, and whole-body PET scans revealed elevated muscle and bone marrow activity in patients.

## Discussion

The disappointing null results of the major controlled trials suggest that broad, universal treatments may be ineffective for a heterogeneous patient population. These outcomes emphasize the critical necessity of patient stratification and biomarker-led trial designs. Conversely, the specific localization of genetic risk in the brain and systemic bioenergetic anomalies detected via PET scans present fresh, promising research avenues.

## Conclusion & Future Work

While the 2026 Berlin conference did not deliver an immediate cure, it represented a vital phase of scientific maturity. Future research must shift away from broad, non-specific treatments and focus instead on precise diagnostic subtyping and targeted clinical trials.
