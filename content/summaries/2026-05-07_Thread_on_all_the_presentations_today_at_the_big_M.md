## Summary

This conference report offers crucial orientation value for patients and caregivers by demonstrating powerful momentum and convergence in ME/CFS research. Rather than focusing on a single breakthrough, it validates that multiple independent global teams are successfully mapping the same physiological disruptions, particularly in neuroinflammation and mitochondrial energy metabolism. This systematic mapping of disease mechanisms provides a strong foundation for future biomarker-led clinical trials and objective diagnostic tools.

## What was researched?

This social media thread summarizes the Day 1 presentations of the International ME/CFS Conference 2026 in Berlin, covering the latest findings in genetics, neuroimaging, immunology, and metabolic dysfunction.

## Why was it researched?

The live coverage and conference aim to disseminate cutting-edge scientific progress directly to patients, caregivers, and clinicians to bridge the gap between complex research and public understanding.

## How was it researched?

The author compiled live-reported summaries of 19 presentations from leading international researchers, featuring data from major initiatives like DecodeME, advanced neuroimaging studies (such as TSPO-PET and 31P-MRS), and immunometabolic trials.

## What has been found?

Key presentations showed neural tissue enrichment of genetic signals, distinct autoimmune endotypes, and brain neuroinflammation evidenced by TSPO-PET and white matter MRI abnormalities. Researchers also reported impaired brain energy metabolism (reduced ATP/PCr ratios), altered tryptophan-indole and polyamine metabolism, and distinct lactate recovery trajectories after brief exertion.

## Discussion

As a conference summary of mostly ongoing projects, the presented findings represent cutting-edge, preliminary data rather than fully peer-reviewed publications. Its major value lies in providing a rapid, high-level overview of global research synergy.

## Conclusion & Future Work

The collective findings strongly support a somatic, multi-systemic pathology for ME/CFS and Long COVID. Future research is focused on translating these neuroimaging, metabolic, and immunological biomarkers into validated diagnostic scores and targeted therapies.
